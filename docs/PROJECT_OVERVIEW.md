# Career Guidance - Project Overview

## Context

Each year in Togo, thousands of high school students and new baccalaureate graduates face one of the most decisive choices of their lives: their university major. Yet the vast majority navigate this entirely blind — without reliable guidance, often swayed by peer pressure or paralyzed by fear of making the wrong choice.

## Problem

New baccalaureate graduates and high school students critically lack a personalized and accessible academic and professional guidance system. Currently, many choose their university programs by mimicry, by default, or due to ignorance of future-oriented professions, without considering their true aptitudes. The consequences are severe: high failure rates in the first years of university, discouragement among youth, and a profound mismatch between graduates and the country's actual human resource needs.

## Solution

**Career Guidance** is an intelligent web platform for academic and professional guidance. Designed for high school students and baccalaureate graduates seeking direction, it acts as a digital academic advisor. Its primary function is to analyze the student's personal profile and recommend professional paths that truly match them, while providing the exact roadmap and local institutions to get there.

## Key Features

1. **Intelligent Onboarding & Profiling** — Interactive form capturing the student's level, passions, interests, and hobbies.
2. **AI-Powered Recommendations** — Generative AI algorithm analyzes the profile and suggests relevant, viable careers.
3. **Dynamic Roadmaps** — For each chosen career, the system generates a detailed action plan including key skills to acquire, priority subjects, and partnering institutions/universities.
4. **Interactive Chatbot** — 24/7 conversational assistant that clearly and kindly answers the student's specific questions (admission requirements, career prospects, etc.).
5. **Personal Space (Authentication)** — Allows each user to save their simulations, track their progress, and update their profile.

## Target Audience

- High school students (lycéens) — Togo
- New baccalaureate graduates (nouveaux bacheliers) — Togo

## Theme

**"Bâtir sa Nation"** (Building the Nation) — Hackathon context.

## Expected Impact

- Drastically reduce academic aimlessness among youth.
- Provide students with a clear vision of their future and precise steps to achieve it.
- Reduce university dropout and re-orientation rates.
- Help align trained graduates with the real needs of the national labor market.
- Ensure equal access to orientation information for all students, regardless of geographic or financial situation.
